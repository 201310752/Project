Enabling those who have something powerful to say and the authority to be heard.

    Lumpy skin disease

    Description
    Lumpy skin disease is poxviral disease primarily of cattle that is spread by biting insects.
    Symptoms
    There is hemorrhagic exudate subjacent to the necrotic center (sitfast) of a papule.
    Multiple subcutaneous nodules elevate the skin.
    There are multiple sharply-demarcated slightly raised papules, often with eroded surfaces, that extend into the nares.
    Medication
    LUMPYVAX® - For the prophylactic immunisation of cattle against lumpy skin disease.
    Recommendation for use:
    If possible all animals should be vaccinated during spring. Calves from vaccinated cows should be vaccinated at 6 months of age. Calves from unvaccinated cows may be vaccinated at any age. Pregnant cows may be vaccinated with Lumpyvax®. A booster vaccination should be given annually.

    Bluetongue disease

    Description
    Bluetongue is a non-contagious, viral disease spread by biting insects. It aﬀects species of ruminants, particularly sheep.
    Symptoms
    Bluetongue causes many diﬀerent symptoms in aﬀected animals, including ulcers, sores, painful hooves, lameness and reproductive problems. Aﬀected animals can have swelling of the tongue (“bluetongue”) which can cause breathing difculties
    Medication
    BioBio BTV 8 - For the prophylactic immunisation of sheep and goats against Bluetongue.
    Recommendation for use:
    If possible all animals should be vaccinated during spring. Calves from vaccinated cows should be vaccinated at 6 months of age. Calves from unvaccinated cows may be vaccinated at any age. Pregnant cows may be vaccinated. A booster vaccination should be given annually.
